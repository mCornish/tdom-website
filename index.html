The Author

My name is Mike Cornish. I'm a compulsive creator. When I'm not writing stories or blog posts, I design and develop websites, draw and sketche art, and occasionally create music. His main passion is learning, because it allows him to execute bigger, better, more ambitious creations.

Some of my most notable accompliishments include working on a Best in Show award-winning website, writing two novels, playing a self-written piano piece before an audience of strangers, interviewing a chart-topping musician, and writing hundreds of blog posts over the past six years.

I am writing The Dawn of Man in order to tell a story about human strength and weakness. Yes, it is a fantasy novel with magic and horror, but it also examines topics like kinship, xenophobia, and mortality. I hope that The Dawn of Man will take you on an adventure through a new world, but that it will also compel you to analyze the world we live in.

Frequently Ask Questions

What is The Dawn of Man?

Four years ago, I started working on my first ever comic book script called The Cleansing. Through many leaps in logic and creativity, that comic book script eventually turned into a novel, The Dawn of Man. It finished writing it about three years ago and am currently in the process of rewriting with a more immersive setting, deeper characters, and an overall more compelling story.

How can I help get The Dawn of Man published?

I'm currently working to get The Dawn of Man published through Inkshares campaign. In order for that to happen, I need at least 250 people to pre-order the novel by November 23. For $10, you can pre-order a digital copy and for $20 you can pre-order a signed paperback copy. I still have a long way to go, but I have some plans for how to get there, including the website you're viewing right now!

Who will enjoy reading The Dawn of Man?

I'm a huge geek who loves comic books like The Walking Dead, sci-fi adventures like Ready Player Oneand Ender's Game, fantasy journies like Assassin's Apprentice and The Hobbit, and horror stories like those written by Stephen King and H.P. Lovecraft. The Dawn of Man is inspired by the type of storytelling and writing in these kinds of stories, but I would narrow the genre down to "fantasy with horror-like inclinations". I'll let you draw your own conclusions from there.
